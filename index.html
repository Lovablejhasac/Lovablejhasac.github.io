01 About

Architecture is not a diagram.
It's a promise kept in production.

I'm a Senior Cloud Architect at Celebal Technologies — a leading Microsoft partner — where I lead the DC & DB Migration Practice. My core work is moving complex, business-critical estates into the cloud, with Azure VMware Solution and HCX as the backbone, and doing it in a way the business can actually trust.

Beyond infrastructure, I build with AI. Project Brain — my flagship product work — applies the same architectural thinking to knowledge itself: how an organization captures what it knows, connects it, and puts it to work.

I operate best as a bridge — translating between executive intent and engineering reality. As a Microsoft Certified Trainer, I do that in the open: workshops, sessions, and reviews that leave teams sharper than I found them.

06 Case Study

Migration Intelligence Copilot:
memory as infrastructure.

Migration teams don't lack intelligence — they lack recall. The analysis that de-risked the last program lives in slide decks, ticket threads, and the heads of people who have moved on. Every new wave pays the same tax: re-deriving decisions that were already made.

The copilot treats that memory as infrastructure. Project documents are ingested and semantically indexed; questions come back as retrieval-grounded answers with citations to the source — never unattributed confidence. Past decisions, tradeoffs, and risks become a queryable asset instead of tribal knowledge.

It's deliberately local-first and free-first, on an open-source stack — so the knowledge stays close to the team that owns it, data never has to leave the room, and the cost of adopting it is zero.

The build itself is part of the thesis: a Claude Code–assisted workflow and a small, sharply scoped MVP plan — built to ship publicly and earn its next feature, the way a migration earns its next wave.

The system, not the model

  1. Ingestion Documents, decisions, and artifacts brought into one indexed store.
  2. Semantic search pgvector embeddings retrieve by meaning, not keyword.
  3. Grounded answers Every response cites the document it came from.
  4. Memory Decisions and rationale persist for reuse across programs.
  5. Trust & evaluation Answers are measured; uncertainty is surfaced, not hidden.
  6. Workflow Designed around how migration teams actually work.
07 AI & Product Thinking

Infrastructure taught me how AI products should be built.

Enterprise infrastructure teaches you one thing above all: systems fail at the seams. The same is true of AI products. The model is rarely the hard part — the hard part is everything around it: retrieval, memory, orchestration, trust, evaluation, and workflow design. That's where AI products are actually won or lost.

So I build AI the way I plan migrations. Define the target state. Map the dependencies. Design for the failure case before the happy path. Ship in waves, validate relentlessly, and never let a demo write a check the production system can't cash.

That's the thesis behind Project Brain and the Migration Intelligence Copilot — and the lens I bring to any conversation about AI in the enterprise: strategic about where it creates value, unsentimental about where it doesn't.

i

Architecture before intelligence — retrieval and data contracts decide whether AI is useful.

ii

Design for the failure case first — trust is the product.

iii

Ship like a migration — in waves, validated, reversible.

iv

Local-first by default — knowledge stays close to the team that owns it.
